\mainhead{Executive Summary} Level II NDT Inspector and Level II Rope Access Technician with around five years of field inspection experience across onshore/offshore Oil \& Gas, shipyards, mining sites and industrial environments. Experienced in applying multiple NDT methods including Eddy Current, Magnetic Particle, Liquid Penetrant, Ultrasonic Thickness Gauging and UT Flaw detection to identify defects and support integrity management. Conducts inspections on vessels and offshore units, producing client-ready technical reports aligned with drawings, codes and standards. Comfortable working on rotation/shift patterns and in physically demanding environments, with a strong focus on safe execution and reliable delivery. Has performed additional mechanical and lifting-related checks such as bolt torque and lifting gear inspections as part of offshore assignments.  \par 
\vspace{8pt} 
\mainhead{Key Achievements} 
\begin{itemizea}  \item \textbf{Multi-method NDT capability:} Qualified and practiced across ET (Level II), MT (Level II), PT (Level II), Wall Thickness Gauging (Level II) and UT Flaw (Level I) for onshore and offshore inspection scopes.
\item \textbf{Offshore and marine inspection delivery:} Completed vessel and offshore-unit inspection campaigns covering hull plates, sea chests, propeller blades, tanks, hatches and structural components, producing physical/isometric drawing-based inspection records and reports.
\item \textbf{Integrity and condition reporting:} Performed inspections of drilling, subsea, marine and hydraulic equipment (e.g., BOP, cranes, tubulars, pressure vessels, pipelines) and issued technical findings and condition/recommendation reports.
\item \textbf{Rope access-enabled inspection:} Applied IRATA rope access capability to support inspection work on onshore and offshore platforms where access constraints required rope access methods.  
\end{itemizea}
